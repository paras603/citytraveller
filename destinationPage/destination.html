Seasonal Highlights

Pokhara

Pokhara is a city in central Nepal situated on Phewa Lake. It is regarded as the entry point to the well-trod Annapurna Circuit in the Himalayas. On an island in the lake lies the two-story pagoda known as the Tal Barahi Temple. There are restaurants and yoga centers in the Lakeside neighborhood on the eastern shore. The International Mountain Museum, located in the southern part of the city, features displays about the history of mountaineering and the Himalayan people.

Annapurna Circuit Trek

A walk around the middle Nepalese mountain ranges is known as the Annapurna Circuit. The route's overall duration ranges from 160 to 230 km, depending on where the walk ends and where motorized transit is utilized. This hike circles the Annapurna Massif and passes through two distinct river valleys.

Kathmandu Valley

The Kathmandu Valley, sometimes called the Nepal Valley or Nepa Valley, is a bowl-shaped valley in Nepal's Himalayan mountains that is referred to as the National Capital Area.

Upper Mustang Trekking

The 14-day, high-adventurous Upper Mustang Trek in the isolated Trans-Himalayan Region of this semi-independent Tibetan Kingdom provides breathtaking trekking through a dramatic, semi-arid desert with snow-capped peaks, deep ravines, and colorful rock formations all around you. The breathtaking beauty is flawlessly complemented with the history, culture, mythology, and legend.

Everest Base Camp

About 40,000 tourists a year leave Lukla Airport (2,846 m or 9,337 ft) to travel to the south side of Everest Base Camp, which is one of the most popular trekking routes in the Himalayas at an elevation of 5,364 m (17,598 ft). In order to save time and energy, trekkers typically take a plane from Kathmandu to Lukla before starting their hike to the base camp. Trekking to Lukla is feasible, though. Since there are no roads connecting Kathmandu with Lukla, the only way to move big, bulky items is by air.

Gosaikunda Lake

Gosaikunda, sometimes written Gosainkunda, is an alpine freshwater oligotrophic lake with a surface size of 13.8 ha (34 acres), situated in the Rasuwa District of Nepal's Langtang National Park at an elevation of 4,380 m (14,370 ft).
